From 2012 to 2018, the NYC Department of Cultural Affairs steadily increased program funding

By Vanessa Ague

November 12, 2020

Between 2012 and 2018, the New York City Department of Cultural Affairs saw a steady stream of budget increases. This led to a continued increase in the amount of program funding awarded to cultural institutions across New York City—a total increase of approximately 30% over the course of six years.

The Bronx Council on the Arts, a multidisciplinary grantmaking institution, saw their awards from the Department of Cultural Affairs increase by approximately 30% over the six year period, which allowed them to distribute more grants to artists and organizations located in the Bronx and to spearhead four different grantmaking initiatives.

“It was great, but you can always use more,” says Executive Director of the Bronx Council on the Arts Viviana Bianchi.

Organizations that saw the greatest increase in funding were folk arts, visual arts & photography and literature; funding for folk arts organizations doubled during the six years.
